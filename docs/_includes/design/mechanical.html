Mechanical Design

Overall assembly

The final class robot is a compact human-carrying hexapod built around a load-bearing chassis, rider support surface, protected electronics, auxiliary support elements, and six independently driven Klann-linkage leg modules. The final report treats the class version as a successful feasibility platform rather than a production stage device: locomotion and navigation worked, but the physical design still needs reliability testing and stage-specific validation.

Mechanically, the project moved from a linkage concept to a serviceable integrated robot: fabricated aluminum structure, individually driven leg modules, packaged electronics, and final cover panels. The final assembly emphasizes maintainable access to the power and compute hardware while preserving a cleaner exterior for performance use.

Klann linkage design

The implemented robot uses one independently commanded RS485 servo motor per leg, not one mechanically coupled shaft per side. Each motor drives a TG-series 60 mm 50:1 planetary gearbox and one Klann crank. This preserves a mechanically encoded stance/swing trajectory while giving software direct control over each leg phase.

The linkage model is measured and reused by the visualizer, body estimator, and control stack. For each crank phase, it returns foot position, foot velocity, and stance confidence, enabling lookup-table evaluation during runtime. Fabrication and single-linkage test media are kept on the Implementation page, where they describe manufacturing and test sequence rather than design intent.
