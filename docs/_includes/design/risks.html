Risks & Mitigations

R1 — RS485 bus latency and encoder wrap ambiguity

The servo encoders are 17-bit single-turn motor encoders. Because the 50:1 gearbox makes one output crank revolution span 50 motor revolutions, raw encoder values wrap many times per output revolution. If polling is too slow relative to motor RPM, the host cannot reliably infer the wrap count.

  • Mitigation: keep runtime RS485 at bounded RPM, use strict six-drive encoder sweeps, changed-only RPM writes, stop-command preemption, ESP timing fields, and host-side continuous unwrap using command history.
  • Future improvement: move to higher-throughput motor networking such as CAN and/or multi-turn absolute encoders.
R2 — Foot slip and contact uncertainty

The kinematic odometry assumes stance feet impose no-slip constraints. False stance classification, foot slip, uneven ground, or compliant surfaces can bias body velocity and yaw estimates.

  • Mitigation: phase-derived stance confidence, conservative speed limits, calibration from tape-measured motion, and ORB-SLAM fusion when camera tracking is valid.
  • Future improvement: add conditioned, calibrated foot-contact sensing with hysteresis and per-foot confidence before using FSR data as an odometry gate.
R3 — Camera occlusion and stage lighting

Riders, performers, and lighting changes can corrupt a forward D415 view. Sparse ORB map points are also not the same as a navigation-grade occupancy grid.

  • Mitigation: treat ORB-SLAM as localization/mapping input, produce a separate virtual scan for slam_toolbox, keep kinematic odometry as a fallback, and expose map save/load in the operator interface for pre-performance map preparation.
R4 — Mechanical fabrication and tolerance accumulation

The final report identifies linkage fabrication as a major schedule and risk driver. Hole alignment, custom-angle components, CNC access, and tolerance accumulation affected assembly more than early CAD models suggested.

  • Mitigation: single-leg prototyping, wooden validation prototypes, measured linkage YAML, RViz/Desmos geometry comparison, and final direct testing under payload.
  • Future improvement: revise leg orientation and consider eight-bar or higher-order walking linkages with flatter stance trajectories and lower vertical body excitation.
R5 — Performance-readiness beyond the class final

The final class version demonstrated the critical feasibility of locomotion and navigation, but future stage deployment would require longer reliability trials, richer safety validation, and environment-specific calibration.

  • Mitigation: retain conservative operating modes, keep manual override available, validate with real stage surfaces/lighting, log odometry and command timing, and separate class-final success from production-readiness claims.
