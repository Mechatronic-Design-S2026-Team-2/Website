Overall assembly

The final class robot is a compact human-carrying hexapod built around a load-bearing chassis, rider support surface, protected electronics, auxiliary support elements, and six Klann-linkage leg modules. The final report treats the class version as a successful feasibility platform rather than a production stage device: locomotion and navigation worked, but the physical design still needs reliability testing and stage-specific validation.

Planned final CAD uploads should be placed at docs/assets/designs/v5_final_assembly.glb for the full assembly and docs/assets/designs/klann_final.glb for the final leg/linkage export. Those files are embedded through <model-viewer> once their pending flags are cleared.

Klann linkage assembly
{% include model-card.html wrap="false" file="/assets/designs/klann-leg.glb" caption="Earlier Klann-linkage GLB. Replace or supplement with /assets/designs/klann_final.glb when the final export is available." %}

The implemented robot uses one independently commanded RS485 servo motor per leg, not one mechanically coupled shaft per side. Each motor drives a TG-series 60 mm 50:1 planetary gearbox and one Klann crank. This preserves a mechanically encoded stance/swing trajectory while giving software direct control over each leg phase.

The linkage model is measured and reused by the visualizer, body estimator, and control stack. For each crank phase, it returns foot position, foot velocity, and stance confidence, enabling lookup-table evaluation during runtime.

Fabrication proof point

The first assembled linkage and finished crank/elbow photos should be placed near the linkage description because they make the CAD-to-fabrication transition explicit. The final report identifies fabrication tolerance, hole alignment, and custom component geometry as major schedule drivers.
